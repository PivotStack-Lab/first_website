Day Four: Broke

How I got here

It had all come to a point. I was crystal clear and needed change. and the change was to go back, not forward. I remembered playing with my dad's computer when I was 12 years old. The World Wide Web was celebrating it's first birthday. I was trying to parition the hard drive. I succeeded!

I realised that I had the confidence, mindset and skills to now have a strong chance of success at making a career in the tech sector. So I started to use those skills and researched the best ways to get a free education. I found The Odin Project.

I was feeling confident. I had done this kind of thing before. I knew HTML boilerplate, how hard could it be!? I had installed a virtual machine to run linux inside my windows machine. It was clunky, and I was feeling good, so I thought 'why not partition the hard drive? Dual booting worked for me before and it'll work for me again.

Am I the drama?

The Odin Project website made it sound pretty simple. And it was at first. Make some space on the drive. Make a flash drive. Reboot the computer and boot from it. It did not work out that way. I couldn't get windows to make space on the drive, according to Google this was due to it storing a number of hidden files on there. All I had to do was download this other software, use it to make space and bingo.

Following the steps I come to a screen showing my laptop booting into repair mode. Oh dear. I watch as it starts to cycle and reboot itself. Over and over again. Can Gemini help me? I ask it to give me some ideas, and it has a few! We're back in business.

OH GOD THE FLASH DRIVE AND PARTITION ARE BROKEN. Business was bad that day. I needed help, or at least a computer I could use to make a working flash drive. I called my friend who, despite his illness, allowed me to come to his home. The fact that it was the Saturday morning of Easter bank holiday weekend, so almost certainly very inconvenient, was never mentioned by him. I will be eternally grateful. Until I forget at least.

Am I ever going to dance again?

At my friends house I was able to create a working flash drive. I bought a new HP one for the occasion. Fancy. I was also lucky enough to get to see the haul of Pokemon gifts they'd received from our friends trip to Japan. I am now saving for a trip to Japan. It looks amazing.

Using the new flash drive, I was finally able to get into a working OS. I had been looking up the price of new laptops while waiting for all this to load. Seeing a different screen to that repair screen of death was the best feeling. But wait! There's more! While trying to install the OS, not just run it off the flash drive, there was yet another road block.

Secure in the knowledge I could get a working OS on there, I headed back home to continue the work. I was felt that I was having to fight this thing every step of the way. I was determined to win though, and I was nearly there. I found the final hick up in the BIOS settings that was preventing me from writing to the disk. And Wham! The basic install compeleted, I boot strapped myself up, it was done.

It's done, Sam

5 hours after bricking my laptop, I was running a perfectly installed version of Linux. I could customise and play to my hearts content. The young version of me, the one who watched Hackers and wanted so badly to be in that world, was having the time of his life. I beat the computer.

Now I'm on day five of working through The Odin Project and am thinking forward to my budding career in tech. How many more hours will I spend debugging and fixing problems? Hundreds? Thousands? Millions?

I think I know the answer.
